Applies to

  • Web Applications

What to Do

Log files should be removed from production servers on a regular basis.&nbsp; The frequency of removal is dependent upon your application's level of activity.&nbsp; Your design should consider the way that log files will be retrieved and moved to offline servers for analysis.&nbsp; Any additional protocols and ports opened on the Web server for this purpose must be securely locked down.

Why

There's no point in logging activity if the log files are never analyzed.

When

Log files should be archived regularly to ensure that they do not fill up or start to cycle, and they should be regularly analyzed to detect signs of intrusion.

How
