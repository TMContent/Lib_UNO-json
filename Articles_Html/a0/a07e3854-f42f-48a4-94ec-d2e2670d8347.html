Applies to

  • PCI DSS Requirement 2.1.1.d
  • PCI DSS Requirement 4.1.1

What to Do

Configure wireless networks to use strong encryption to protect authentication and network traffic.

Why

Strong encryption and authentication protocols for wireless communications prevent both unauthorized access to the network and eavesdropping on wireless communications. An earlier implementation of wireless encryption and authentication, known as WEP, has proven to be insecure.

How

Configure the wireless access point(s) to use strong encryption and authentication protocols, such as WPA or WPA2. The detailed instructions for enabling strong encryption and authentication vary by wireless access points and network designs. Please refer to the vendor's documentation or technical support for specific instructions on enabling strong encryption and authentication protocols on your wireless network(s).
