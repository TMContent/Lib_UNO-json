What to Check For

Ensure that your application monitors all privileged accounts.

Why

Privileged accounts by their nature are allowed to do potentially damaging activities. If compromised, they can cause the most damage. Close monitoring can catch unauthorized and/or malicious activities and help minimize any damage done.

How to Check

To check if your application monitors privileged accounts:

  1. Identify the privileged users. For the purposes of security monitoring, privileged accounts are those that can cause more damage than other users. This will vary depending on your application and its internally defined user roles, however, some examples are:

    • Administrator
    • Power user
    • Team leader

  2. Audit the logs. If there are no logs available to audit, use your application to generate enough logged sample data from the application's components that require privileged accounts. During the log audit, mark and categorize the information that has been logged.

  3. Verify that your application monitors privileged accounts. Identify all logged privileged events. Ensure that your application logs all privileged events as identified in your application's design and requirements. Common privileged events include:

    • Project management
    • User management
    • Global application management

Note: PCI DSS guidelines require that actions by users with root or administrative privileges be logged and that logs be kept for at least one year.

How to Fix

To monitor privileged accounts, use the following steps:

  1. Identify the privileged users. For the purposes of security monitoring, privileged accounts are those that can cause more damage than other users. This will vary by application and roles defined, however some examples are:

    • Administrator
    • Power user
    • Team leader

  2. Ensure there are auditing mechanisms in place. Place logging mechanisms on functionality that is unique to privileged users. This will ensure there is a record of administrative activities by privileged users. Examples of such areas include:

    • Project management
    • User management
    • Global application management

Note: PCI DSS requirements state that administrator actions should be logged and that logs should be checked at least once each day.

Java Logging Guidelines

  • Centralize Logging
  • Protect Log Files
  • Log Important Event Details
  • Do Not Log Sensitive Information
  • Log Important Security Operations
  • Log Important Business Operations
  • Monitor the Use of Privileged Accounts
  • Log Unusual Activity
  • Consider Log Throttling

Java Logging Checklists

  • Logging Is Centralized
  • Log Files Are Protected
  • Important Event Details Are Logged
  • Sensitive Data Is Not Logged
  • Important Security Operations Are Logged
  • Important Business Operations Are Logged
  • Use of Privileged Accounts Is Monitored
  • Unusual Activity Is Logged
  • Logging Is Throttled
