What to Check For

The PAN should be rendered unreadable whenever it is stored.

Why

The PAN should be rendered unreadable whenever it is stored to prevent disclosure in the event of a successful compromise.

How to Check

Identify if and where the PAN is stored. Examine a sample of database tables or files to verify that PAN is not stored in plain-text. Examine a sample of removable media that contains PAN information, if any, to confirm that the PAN is rendered unreadable. Examine a sample of the system logs to verify that the PAN is rendered unreadable or removed from the logs. Identify the mechanism used to secure the PAN. The acceptable mechanisms are: one-way crypto-hashes, truncation, index tokens and pads, and strong cryptography.

How to Fix

Identify if and where the PAN is stored. Choose one of the following mechanisms to protect the PAN: one-way crypto-hashes, truncation, index tokens and pads, and strong cryptography. Implement the chosen mechanism.
